What is Energy Resources?

Energy resources refer to the materials or processes that can be used to generate power. They can be renewable, such as solar, wind, and hydropower, or non-renewable, like coal, oil, and natural gas. Renewable energy resources are considered more sustainable since they are replenished naturally over time. Non-renewable resources, on the other hand, are finite and can lead to environmental harm when depleted. The use of energy resources is crucial for economic development, but it also raises concerns about environmental impact and the need for alternative energy solutions.

Why is Energy Resources important?

Energy resources are important because they power our homes, schools, and transportation. They also support economic growth by fueling industries and businesses. Hospitals and health services also rely on energy for equipment and to store medicines safely. Renewable energy can also reduce pollution and fight climate change. A reliable energy supply improves safety and a better sustainable future for everyone.

Problems in Achieving Clean Energy

  • Many remote areas still lack access to reliable electricity.
  • Overdependence on fossil fuels causes high carbon emissions.
  • High initial costs of renewable energy systems.
  • Lack of awareness and government support in some regions.

Clean Energy Recommendations

About Us

SYNERGY is a youth-led initiative advocating for clean and sustainable energy in the Philippines. Our mission is to promote and encourage communities to practice and develop a systematic and clean way of harnessing energy.
